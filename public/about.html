What defines a Haiku?

Where did Haiku originate?

Haiku was developed by the venerated poet Matsuo "Banana Tree" Bashō in 17th Century Edo era Japan. Rooted in more traditional forms of collaborative poetry, Bashō used haiku to chronicle his hermit journeys across the medieval Japanese country side.

Rules for a Haiku:

  1. Kiru: “cutting” wording that juxtapose two opposing images

  2. Maximum of 17 syllables metered in 3 verses and distributed as 5-7-5

  3. Kigo: words intended to evoke nostalgic feelings associated with seasons or holidays

What does Haiku look like?

The most famous haiku was written in Bashō in 1686:

The Old Pond

An old silent pond...
A frog jumps into the pond,
splash! Silence again.
-Bashō
